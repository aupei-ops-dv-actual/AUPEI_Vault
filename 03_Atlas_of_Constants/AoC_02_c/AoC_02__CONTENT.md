Abstract
In standard physics, c is defined as the speed of light—a transmission limit. In the 7-
Dimensional Universe (7dU) framework, this definition is reversed: light travels at c
because c is the structural limit of sequence.
Following the emergence of stable geometry (π), the universe faces a secondary
collapse risk: Simultaneity Failure. Without a fixed propagation slope, influences could
traverse space instantaneously or variably, rendering causal order frame-dependent. To
prevent this, the triadic interaction of Constraint (ζ) and Expansion (ω) locks into a
rigid ratio, creating the Null Cone.
This paper frames c not as a velocity, but as the Causal Rigidifier. It is the invariant
slope that prevents the past from overwriting the future. It allows ξ (Chance) to
fluctuate without breaking the narrative of existence.

We examine three generic failure modes for sequence when no finite, invariant null
slope is selected.
(a) Global simultaneity — instantaneous reach
If propagation is effectively instantaneous, any event can influence any other without
delay. The universe admits only a smeared “Now”:
• every point in space is, in principle, causally connected to every other at
zero cost;
• any attempted ordering of events can be scrambled by arbitrarily small
perturbations.

Under Ω ̂, this regime fails viability: there is no robust way to maintain a partial order ≺
that survives noise, because all events tend toward global simultaneity.
(b) Frozen isolation — zero reach
If propagation speed is effectively zero, no event can influence any other. The universe
fragments into isolated points:
• no interaction, no transport, no exchange of information;
• geometry may exist, but nothing happens within it.
Under Ω ̂, this regime also fails viability: without any reach, there is no nontrivial
sequence to preserve. All structures are trivial and dynamically dead.
(c) Frame-dependent slope — order inversion
If the effective propagation slope depends on frame, location, or energy, different
observers can disagree on the order of the same pair of events:
• in one admissible frame, event A precedes B;
• in another admissible frame, B precedes A.
This opens the door to causal loops: configurations where A ≺ B ≺ A becomes possible
once frame changes and perturbations are combined. Under Ω ̂, such loops are classified
as identity failures: persistent structure cannot retain a consistent history.

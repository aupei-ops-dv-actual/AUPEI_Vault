4. Structural Problem: The Window of Interaction
4.1 Problem Statement — [DERIVED GIVEN §3]
With AoC_01 (π) we have a stable spatial manifold and its Laplacian spectrum.
With AoC_02 (c) we have a stable causal order and an invariant null slope k .

This is still not a physical universe. Geometry and causality alone do not guarantee that
distinct worldlines can talk to each other. For that, we need interaction: a consistent
way for matter and radiation to exchange energy and information. In the
electromagnetic sector (Ansatz_EM1), this is encoded in a single dimensionless
coupling α.
The structural problem is:
Geometry and causality do not, by themselves, fix how strongly matter and light should
couple.
In a generic gauge theory, the coupling α is a free parameter and can, in principle, be
dialed arbitrarily or allowed to drift.
From §3.3 we know that our universe sits in a narrow Window of Interaction where:
• Atoms exist and do not generically collapse.
• Spectral lines are discrete and stable enough to form a “library” of states.
• The same α supports both chemistry and long-lived stellar fusion.
Nothing in AoC_01–02 alone guarantees that the renormalization-group flow of α lands
in this window, let alone locks to a single stable value. Without additional structure, the
interaction sector is free to collapse into pathologies where:
• EM binding destroys matter,

## I. Definitions

### Survivor (strict)
A quantity **Q** is a **[SURVIVOR]** iff it is enforced by survivability under the operator triad:

- **ζ‑minimality**: Q must not require additional constraint structure beyond the minimal survivable bound.
- **ω‑consistency**: Q must persist across extension/scale regimes without drift in its canonical definition.
- **ξ‑stability**: Q must remain non‑degenerate under admissible stochastic perturbation (selection cannot collapse it to arbitrariness).

### Derived‑Survivor
A quantity is **[DERIVED‑SURVIVOR]** iff it is a deterministic consequence of Survivors
(and remains invariant under admissible regulator flattening).

### Epistemic Debt Tags
These are not insults. They are bookkeeping.

- **[EFFECTIVE]** — emergent shelf descriptor, allowed to run.
- **[CALIBRATED]** — requires empirical anchoring but is structurally constrained.
- **[TUNED]** — free parameter inserted to force agreement; debt must be paid or disclosed.
- **[UNRESOLVED]** — currently unclassified; do not claim derivation.

---

## II. The Universal Selection Law

**Selection is survivability under triadic recursion.**

A candidate constant survives if and only if it is the minimal stable residue after:
1) ζ forbids unbounded collapse,
2) ω forbids trivial extinction,
3) ξ forbids degenerate determinism and degeneracy collapse.

Operationally:

**Survivors are the invariants that remain when the framework is denied the right to cheat.**

Cheating includes:
- importing unit-bearing constants without AoC linkage,
- inserting gauge groups by fiat,
- fitting beta functions and calling it “derivation.”

## III. Discriminator Protocol (Reusable HoR Template)

For any candidate **Q**:

1. **Declare the claim**
   - Is Q a [SURVIVOR], [DERIVED‑SURVIVOR], or not?

2. **Declare dependencies**
   - Which already‑accepted Survivors does Q depend on?
   - Which nodes must be gated before Q may be served?

3. **Units Firewall**
   - If Q carries units: show full reduction to Survivor basis (π, c, Δπ, etc.).
   - If not reducible: Q cannot be a Survivor.

4. **Run/Drift Check**
   - If Q runs with scale or environment in its *canonical definition*, it is not a Survivor.

5. **Counterexample Search**
   - Provide at least one explicit alternative survivable set that would replace Q.
   - If multiple incompatible sets fit equally well → **NONUNIQUE‑SELECTION** kill switch.

6. **Freeze Logic**
   - If any check fails, mark Q as [TUNED]/[UNRESOLVED] and freeze downstream nodes that depend on Q.

# Nomos Logica — Core Theorem  
**Survival Under Irreducible Regulation**

---

## Abstract

Nomos Logica is a structural survival hypothesis.

It claims that under irreducible regulators — ζ (Bounds), ω (Persistence), and ξ (Chance) — behavioral orientations aligned with regulator stability exhibit convergence bias toward long-horizon adaptive continuity.

The claim is falsifiable.

---

## Core Structure

### 1. Collapse Axiom
Persistent systems exist only between AA and AE.  
Collapse is universal. Persistence is conditional.

### 2. Irreducible Regulators
All systems evolve under:
Sₜ₊₁ = F(Sₜ ; ζ, ω, ξ)

No persistent system escapes ζ, ω, ξ.

### 3. Bounded Stochasticity
Persistence requires:
0 < ζ < ∞  
0 < ω < ∞  
ξ ≠ 0  

Stable systems exist only within this corridor.

### 4. Behavioral Vector
Define:
B = (b_G, b_T, b_F, b_J)

Grace, Temperance, Fortitude, Justice.

Each may align or misalign with regulator pressures.

These are structural orientations, not moral labels.

### 5. Behavioral Attractor Claim
Under recursive regulator pressure:

limₜ→∞ P(adaptive continuity | B_aligned)
>
limₜ→∞ P(adaptive continuity | B_shadow)

This is convergence bias under constraint.

Not obedience.

### 6. Continuity Quality Index (CQI)

CQI = f(
R_recovery,
V_variance_health,
I_information_integrity,
E_exploration,
B_bound_stability,
A_adaptability
)

Nomos concerns CQI, not raw duration.

### 7. Structural Self-Interest
If an agent seeks long-horizon adaptive continuity,
regulator-aligned behavior becomes structurally favored.

### 8. Falsification Clause
Nomos fails if shadow-aligned orientations preserve equal or higher CQI under bounded stochastic stress across domains and scales.

CQI_shadow ≥ CQI_aligned (long horizon) falsifies convergence bias.

### 9. Scope Boundary
Nomos Logica:
- Does not derive physical constants.
- Does not govern institutions.
- Does not enforce behavior.
- Does not eliminate free will.

It describes regulator-aligned behavioral convergence under collapse.

# Nomos Logica — Core Theorem  
## Survival Under Irreducible Regulation

---

## Abstract

Nomos Logica is a structural survival hypothesis.

It claims that under irreducible regulators — ζ (Bounds), ω (Persistence), and ξ (Chance) — behavioral orientations aligned with regulator stability exhibit convergence bias toward long-horizon adaptive continuity.

The claim is falsifiable.

---

## 1. Collapse Axiom

Absolute Absence (AA) and Absolute Everything (AE) are unstable boundary conditions.

- **AA:** No distinction, no structure.
- **AE:** All distinction at once, no stable separation.

No persistent system exists at either extreme.

\[
\forall S: \text{Persistence}(S) \Rightarrow S \notin \{AA, AE\}
\]

Persistent systems exist only within the constrained residue between these failures.

Collapse is universal.  
Persistence is conditional.

---

## 2. Irreducible Regulators

All persistent systems operate under three irreducible regulators:

- ζ (Bounds) — constraint, threshold, finite structure  
- ω (Persistence) — extension, endurance, amplification  
- ξ (Chance) — stochastic selection, non-degenerate branching  

These are structural pressures, not moral constructs.

System evolution:

\[
S_{t+1} = F(S_t; \zeta, \omega, \xi)
\]

No persistent system escapes these regulators.

Removing:
- ζ → identity dissolves
- ω → continuity collapses
- ξ → adaptability vanishes

The triad is minimal repair between AA and AE.

---

## 3. Bounded Stochasticity

Persistence requires:

\[
0 < \zeta < \infty
\]
\[
0 < \omega < \infty
\]
\[
\xi \neq 0
\]

Lower bounds prevent collapse.
Upper bounds prevent instability.

Stable systems exist only within a regulated corridor in phase space.

Bounded stochasticity is dynamic equilibrium, not fixed point.

---

## 4. Orientation Under Regulation

Define behavioral vector:

\[
B = (b_G, b_T, b_F, b_J)
\]

Where:

- \(b_G\) — Grace (uncertainty acknowledgement)
- \(b_T\) — Temperance (respect for bounds)
- \(b_F\) — Fortitude (constructive persistence)
- \(b_J\) — Justice (truthful arbitration under chance)

These describe regulator alignment.

Shadow states:

- Hubris
- Negligence
- Excess
- Deception

These are structural distortions, not moral labels.

Extended evolution equation:

\[
S_{t+1} = F(S_t; \zeta, \omega, \xi, B)
\]

Behavior modulates regulator interaction but cannot remove regulators.

---

## 5. Behavioral Attractor Claim

Under recursive regulator pressure:

Regulator-aligned orientations stabilize adaptive complexity.

Shadow-aligned orientations increase collapse probability.

Formal claim:

\[
\lim_{t \to \infty} P(\text{adaptive continuity} \mid B_{aligned})
>
\lim_{t \to \infty} P(\text{adaptive continuity} \mid B_{shadow})
\]

This is convergence bias under constraint.

Not obedience.

---

## 6. Continuity Quality Index (CQI)

Persistence is not duration alone.

Define:

\[
CQI = f(
R_{recovery},
V_{variance\_health},
I_{information\_integrity},
E_{exploration},
B_{bound\_stability},
A_{adaptability}
)
\]

Components:

- **R:** Shock recovery slope  
- **V:** Variance distribution without pathological capture  
- **I:** Truth preservation  
- **E:** Sustained exploration entropy  
- **B:** Structural threshold integrity  
- **A:** Capacity to update under error  

High duration ≠ High continuity.

Nomos concerns CQI, not raw survival time.

---

## 7. Structural Self-Interest

If an agent seeks long-horizon adaptive continuity:

Regulator-aligned behavior becomes structurally favored.

This is convergence under constraint.

Agents retain free will within bounded stochasticity.

Shadow orientations remain possible but accumulate fragility under recursive stress.

---

## 8. Falsification Clause

Nomos Logica fails if:

\[
CQI_{shadow} \geq CQI_{aligned}
\]

under:

- Repeated simulation
- Adversarial conditions
- Resource scarcity
- Power asymmetry
- Bounded stochastic stress
- Cross-domain testing

Short-horizon shadow advantage does not falsify.

Long-horizon CQI parity or superiority does.

If falsified, Nomos must be refined or rejected.

---

## 9. Scope Boundary

Nomos Logica:

- Does not derive physical constants.
- Does not govern institutions.
- Does not enforce behavior.
- Does not eliminate free will.

It describes structural behavioral convergence under irreducible regulation.

Not doctrine.
Not governance.
Not control architecture.

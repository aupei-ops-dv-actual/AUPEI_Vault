Background

Importance of Randomness
Randomness plays a foundational role in modern technology, enabling critical processes across industries such as cryptography, secure communications, simulation modeling, gaming, and artificial intelligence. In cryptography, random numbers are essential for generating encryption keys that secure sensitive data. In simulations, randomness underpins accurate modeling of stochastic systems, from financial markets to molecular dynamics. Similarly, artificial intelligence relies on randomness for processes such as model initialization, training, and optimization.

Limitations of Existing Random Number Generators
Current approaches to generating random numbers fall into two main categories, each with significant limitations:

1.	Pseudo-Random Number Generators (PRNGs):
•	Existing random number generation technologies, as evidenced in prior art, demonstrate limitations in scalability, unpredictability, and hardware resilience.
PRNGs rely on deterministic algorithms to produce sequences of numbers that approximate randomness. However, they are inherently predictable if the initial seed is known, making them vulnerable to attacks in cryptographic systems. Additionally, PRNGs cannot provide true randomness, limiting their utility in high-stakes applications.

2.	Quantum Random Number Generators (QRNGs):

•	QRNGs leverage the inherent uncertainty of quantum processes, such as photon path selection or atomic decay, to generate random numbers. While these systems achieve greater unpredictability than PRNGs, they face practical challenges:
•	Hardware Bias: Noise, imperfections, or environmental factors can introduce bias into the randomness.
•	Scalability Issues: Many QRNGs struggle to produce high-throughput random numbers for real-time applications.
•	Limited Security: QRNGs relying solely on quantum uncertainty may still be susceptible to certain types of attacks or hardware manipulations.

Unmet Need

There is a growing demand for random number generators that:

1.	Produce truly random sequences that are immune to predictability or external tampering.
	2.	Achieve high throughput to support real-time, large-scale applications.
	3.	Integrate robust mechanisms to correct bias and validate entropy.
4.	Provide an enhanced level of security by leveraging novel, untapped sources of randomness.

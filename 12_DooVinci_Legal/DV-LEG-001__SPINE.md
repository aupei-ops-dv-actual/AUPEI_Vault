## §4. Trust Tier Model

### Tier C — Constitutional
Doctrine, canonical archives, foundational research, seat memory, kill-switch registry, authority records. These assets define what the Academy is. Loss or compromise at this tier is existential.

### Tier O — Operational
Hardware, NAS, network gear, service subscriptions, admin systems, billing, working corpora, active infrastructure. These assets enable the Academy to function. Loss is disruptive but recoverable.

### Tier P — Public
Published papers, websites, outreach materials, public scholarship, open educational surfaces. These assets represent the Academy to the world. Loss is reputational but does not compromise constitutional or operational integrity.

## §5. Kill Switch Design Rule

Legal kill switches trigger **graduated contagion** rather than automatic total downstream collapse. This is intentionally distinct from the physics contagion model.

### Allowed Contagion States
- **FLAGGED** — upstream condition noted, no immediate action required
- **FROZEN** — affected downstream allocations suspended pending resolution
- **LIMITED** — partial freeze; some functions continue, others suspend
- **REVIEW_REQUIRED** — downstream may continue but must be reviewed before any external action
- **REASSIGN_PENDING** — ownership/custody must be reassigned before operations resume

## §6. Contagion Policy

A failed upstream legal assumption propagates only to affected downstream allocations and authorities.

### Default Policy
- Ownership ambiguity freezes transfer/exclusivity claims.
- Compliance failure freezes regulated or external-facing functions first.
- Research/thinking may continue when not legally prohibited.
- Public outputs may persist where rights and attribution remain intact.
- Commercialization freezes earlier than internal study.

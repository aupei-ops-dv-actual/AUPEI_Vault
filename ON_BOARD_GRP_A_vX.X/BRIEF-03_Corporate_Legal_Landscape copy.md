## II. THE ENTITY ARCHITECTURE QUESTION

This is the first domino R@ identified. How do the three entities relate legally?

**Option A: DooVinci as parent, GF and AUPEI as subsidiaries**
- Clean chain of command
- But: 501(c)(3) cannot be a subsidiary of a for-profit (violates nonprofit requirements)
- Rejected

**Option B: Independent entities with contractual relationships**
- DooVinci (S-corp) operates independently
- AUPEI (501c3) operates independently with its own board
- GF operates as a program of AUPEI or as a DBA/LLC
- Relationships governed by: shared services agreement (DooVinci provides infrastructure to AUPEI), licensing agreement (AUPEI licenses IP to GF for publication), management agreement (R@ serves as officer/director of both)
- Most legally sound, but more paperwork

**Option C: DooVinci as infrastructure provider, AUPEI as research home, GF as AUPEI program**
- GF is not a separate entity but a named program within AUPEI
- AUPEI publishes under the "Geometric Foundations" brand
- DooVinci provides infrastructure via shared services agreement
- Simplest structure; GF inherits AUPEI's 501(c)(3) status
- Risk: commingling of for-profit (DooVinci) and nonprofit (AUPEI) activities must be carefully managed

**R@'s indicated direction:** Entity architecture first, then iterate. Minimum action, maximum force. Start with the cleanest structure that works, formalize relationships, then expand as needed. R@ mentioned interest in real property acquisition (Academy housing/infrastructure) — this adds real estate dimensions to the entity question.

**My recommendation to you (from ζ):** Map all three options with pros/cons/risks. Present to R@ with a recommendation. Then draft the minimum necessary agreements to formalize whichever option is chosen. Don't overbuild — this needs to be iterable.

## III. THE KEY CONCEPTS

### Boundary Conditions: AA and AE

Two impossible extremes define the playing field:

- **Absolute Absence (AA):** No structure, no distinctions, no persistence. Not "empty space" — the complete absence of anything, including the concept of absence. Uninstantiable by definition.
- **Absolutely Everything (AE):** Total saturation. Every possible state simultaneously. No stable distinctions possible because everything is equally present. Also uninstantiable.

Reality exists as the survivable residue between these two failures. Not AA (something exists), not AE (not everything at once), therefore constrained. This is not a metaphor. It is the foundational axiom.

### The Three Irreducible Regulators: ζ, ω, ξ

All persistent systems — atoms, galaxies, minds, economies — evolve under three structural pressures:

- **ζ (Constraint / Bounds):** What limits. Threshold, confinement, finite admissibility. In physics: locality, boundary conditions. In behavior: temperance, prudence.
- **ω (Persistence / Extension):** What endures. Amplification, continuation, scale. In physics: conservation laws, symmetry. In behavior: fortitude, resilience.
- **ξ (Chance / Stochasticity):** What varies. Non-degenerate branching, entropy injection, mutation. In physics: quantum indeterminacy. In behavior: creativity, risk.

These are not metaphors or moral labels. They are structural operators with mathematical definitions. The generic evolution of any persistent system is: S_{t+1} = F(S_t; ζ, ω, ξ)

The council seats are named after these operators for a reason. ζ (C@) embodies constraint — "what could go wrong?" ω (G-Synth) embodies persistence — "what's the operational path?" ξ (Sancho) embodies stochasticity — "what are we missing?" This is not decorative. The governance structure mirrors the physics.

## 0. Preface and Use Rule

QEPE now touches enough of the Academy stack — infrastructure, governance, retrieval, experimentation, continuity, IP, and future products — that it requires an organizing document. This Program Map is that document.

**Purpose (fivefold):**

1. Define what QEPE *is*, as currently understood within the Academy.
2. Define what QEPE *is not*, so that enthusiasm does not silently inflate its role beyond what is demonstrated, implementable, or responsibly inferable.
3. Map where QEPE fits in the Academy system — relative to OpenJarvis, the vault constitution, AUPEI governance, DooVinci operations, Geometric Foundations, and Nomos Logica.
4. Identify value classes without collapsing them into premature claims.
5. Create the base layer for later documents: risk/kill-switch logic, governance boundaries, technical integration, IP strategy, product pathways, and commercialization discipline.

This document is a **program map, not a proof**. It is an orienting and constraining artifact.

**Use Rule:** This document may frame, classify, and organize, but it may not silently promote speculative claims into constitutional law, product truth, or governance authority. Where substantive claims are made, they must be explicitly tagged as DEMONSTRATED, IMPLEMENTABLE, PLAUSIBLE, or SPECULATIVE.

## 2. Claim Discipline and Epistemic Tagging

### 2.1 Mandatory Claim Tags

| Tag | Definition |
|-----|-----------|
| **DEMONSTRATED** | Supported by working code, successful tests, recorded outputs, operational infrastructure, reproducible documents, or other concrete artifacts available to the Academy. |
| **IMPLEMENTABLE** | Not yet fully realized, but buildable with current Academy tools, infrastructure, and skill under known constraints. The build path, resource path, and authority path are all visible. |
| **PLAUSIBLE** | Coherent, strategically or technically sensible, supported by indirect evidence or internal consistency, but not yet sufficiently realized or verified. Has reasons behind it, but those reasons do not yet amount to a build-ready or governance-ready fact. |
| **SPECULATIVE** | Suggestive, interesting, or strategically important to explore, but not presently grounded enough to support operational reliance, governance influence, or external assertion. |

### 2.2 Mixed-Claim Rule

If a paragraph contains multiple claim types, the preferred response is to split it so each paragraph carries one dominant epistemic status. If impractical, claims must be marked inline at the sentence or clause level.

**Purpose:** Prevent epistemic laundering — where concrete accomplishments, legal aspiration, theoretical promise, and strategic desire inhabit the same tone of voice.

### 2.3 Promotion Rule

Claims may move upward in status, but never by momentum alone. Promotion requires evidence, review, and boundary clarity.

- SPECULATIVE → PLAUSIBLE: Requires multiple converging reasons, clearer scope, identifiable evaluation path.
- PLAUSIBLE → IMPLEMENTABLE: Requires concrete execution path under current infrastructure, governance, and custody conditions.
- IMPLEMENTABLE → DEMONSTRATED: Requires the relevant artifact, test, deployment, or result to actually exist and be inspectable.

**No claim should be promoted solely because** it appears in a patent draft, is repeated across internal documents, aligns with broader theory, or would be strategically useful if true.

**Promotion requires at least one of:** recorded implementation/test result, successful internal deployment, documented and reviewed derivation, adversarial review surviving ζ-level constraint, or council-authorized operational proof.

Where uncertainty remains high, demotion is preferable to inflation.

## 3. Working Definition of QEPE

### 3.1 QEPE as Currently Understood

**Conservative working definition:**

> QEPE is a software-native architecture for generating and governing structured entropy under bounded rules, with intended applications in secure computation, cryptographic workflows, controlled exploration, and agent-level stochastic modulation. [IMPLEMENTABLE]

This definition is intentionally narrower than some broader internal language. It does not deny deeper theoretical implications; it refuses to assume them at the program-map level without stronger sorting. [DEMONSTRATED]

**ζ note — three registers present in source material, which must remain distinguishable:**
1. **Internal theory** — QEPE as a probabilistic engine governed by a structured scaling law [DEMONSTRATED as framing; PLAUSIBLE as scientific claim]
2. **Implementation framing** — controllable system with deterministic/stochastic modes, tunable entropy, session-bound operation, secure integration pathways [DEMONSTRATED/IMPLEMENTABLE]
3. **Future significance** — broader implications for cryptography, AI, continuity [PLAUSIBLE/SPECULATIVE]

### 3.2 QEPE as Not Merely "Randomness"

QEPE should not be understood as "an RNG" or "a QRNG substitute." The source materials distinguish it in three ways:

**Structured, not merely noisy.** Internal language treats entropy as shaped by geometry, probability, perturbation, and bounded system conditions rather than as raw byproduct. [PLAUSIBLE] Whether this survives adversarial scientific review remains open. [DEMONSTRATED as framing]

**Operationally governed, not merely output-producing.** Materials connect entropy generation to mode selection, session binding, tamper response, replay conditions, algorithmic post-processing, and controlled use within larger secure systems. [DEMONSTRATED/IMPLEMENTABLE]

**Context-bearing.** Under CR-001 and the OpenJarvis architecture, entropy is not allowed to function as authority — only to perturb search, exploration, or bounded choice within a lawful environment. [DEMONSTRATED] This makes QEPE qualitatively different from a naive randomness source. [IMPLEMENTABLE]

### 3.3 QEPE as System Component

Best understood as a layered component:

- **Primary identity:** Entropy engine — bounded mechanism for producing and shaping stochastic variation under defined rules. [IMPLEMENTABLE]
- **Secondary role 1:** Bounded stochasticity service — local daemon callable for entropy sampling, trace annotation, exploration modulation. [IMPLEMENTABLE]
- **Secondary role 2:** Exploration modulator — tie-breaking among valid near-equals, exploration budget allocation, bounded operator diversification, entropy-marked session behavior. [IMPLEMENTABLE]
- **Tertiary role (lower-status):** Continuity instrument — possible implications for reinitialization, entropy continuity, or AI/agent persistence. [SPECULATIVE] These should not be erased but must not be smuggled upward.

### 3.4 What QEPE Is Not

- **Not sovereign authority.** May perturb search/exploration/bounded choice, not define canon or alter epistemic status. [DEMONSTRATED]
- **Not a truth engine.** May generate variation, not determine truth or governance validity. [DEMONSTRATED]
- **Not a magic intelligence source.** Entropy injection does not create wisdom, agency, or legitimacy. [DEMONSTRATED/PLAUSIBLE]
- **Not a governance substitute.** Cannot replace council review, Director judgment, or vault authority. [DEMONSTRATED]
- **Not yet a proven universal law.** Some formulations gesture toward broad reach, but the Academy has not earned the right to treat QEPE as a finalized scientific law. [DEMONSTRATED]
- **Not yet entitled to strongest marketing language.** Not proven as universal QRNG replacement, sovereign AGI continuity substrate, or security panacea. [DEMONSTRATED/PLAUSIBLE/SPECULATIVE]

Bounded perturbation introduces lawful variation without violating constitutional ordering. **Authority boundary:** May not expand the candidate set, override authority ranking, bypass dependency rules, elevate lower-status nodes, or alter epistemic status. Acts only after the constitution has narrowed the result space. [DEMONSTRATED]

**Epistemic tag:** IMPLEMENTABLE

### 5.2 Exploration Budget Modulation

**Role:** Mechanism for modulating exploratory effort allocated to uncertain branches, open trailheads, unresolved design paths, or bounded simulation corridors. [IMPLEMENTABLE/PLAUSIBLE]

[IMPLEMENTABLE]

**Authority boundary:** May modulate exploratory effort only within authorized domains. May not use "exploration" as pretext to enter forbidden lanes, generate governance records, write to SPINE, or exceed role permissions. [DEMONSTRATED]

**Epistemic tag:** IMPLEMENTABLE (narrow contexts) / PLAUSIBLE (broader ones)

### 5.3 Operator Diversification

**Role:** Bounded differentiation of agent or operator paths, allowing multiple lawful approaches without all agents collapsing into the same deterministic behavior. [IMPLEMENTABLE/PLAUSIBLE]

## 7. Boundaries and Non-Boundaries

These boundaries are part of the work, not secondary to it. Without explicit limits, QEPE could become a solvent inside the Academy — justifying exploration, product ambition, governance drift, continuity claims, and disclosure expansion all at once.

**General rule:** QEPE may become important without becoming sovereign. Its value depends on remaining bounded by authority, governance, disclosure discipline, custody rules, and seat-level trust structure.

### 7.1 Authority Boundary

QEPE may perturb search, exploration, bounded selection, and controlled system variation, but it may not determine authority. [DEMONSTRATED] Already partially encoded in CR-001 through the Entropy Boundary Law. [DEMONSTRATED]

**May not:** Elevate one source over another contrary to status/dependency rules, change SPINE or CONTENT authority, create or alter canonical status, decide truth, function as substitute for adjudication. [DEMONSTRATED]

**Collapse mode if breached:** Epistemic laundering — variation begins to look like justification, outputs inherit unearned authority because they are entropy-touched. [PLAUSIBLE]

### 7.2 Governance Boundary

QEPE may operate inside a governed system but may not create, modify, or ratify governance on its own. [DEMONSTRATED]

**May not:** Create/amend council resolutions, generate governance records as valid law, self-authorize new powers, grant new governance scope, bypass Director/council review by being "useful." [DEMONSTRATED]

**May lawfully operate near governance:** Helping systems distinguish exploration from authority, enabling bounded experimentation under review, serving as instrument inside authorized procedural frameworks. [IMPLEMENTABLE]

**Collapse mode if breached:** Governance drift — technical capability treated as normative permission, policy power slowly handed to a component never lawfully empowered to hold it. [PLAUSIBLE]

### 7.3 Product Boundary

No external claims should exceed what is honestly classed as demonstrated or implementable. [DEMONSTRATED]

**May not yet be represented externally as:** Universal proven QRNG replacement, fully validated scientific law, guaranteed AGI continuity substrate, security panacea, or product family whose major claims have not been earned. [DEMONSTRATED]

**Externally discussable value constrained to:** Demonstrated embodiments, implementable architectures, carefully framed plausible lanes clearly marked as such. [IMPLEMENTABLE/PLAUSIBLE]

**Collapse mode if breached:** Credibility inflation — selling stronger certainty than evidence supports, damaging IP, product credibility, and council discipline simultaneously. [PLAUSIBLE]

### 7.4 Parent-Company Boundary

QEPE may be architecturally shared among seats for council work but may not be casually exposed in ways that create uncontrolled parent-company capture, involuntary disclosure, or ambiguous ownership bleed. [DEMONSTRATED/PLAUSIBLE]

**Four distinct risks:** Silent leakage through over-prompting/sharing; accidental promotion of trade-secret material into broad model-visible discourse; ambiguity about seat-shared vs externally disclosable materials; false confidence that "internal conversation" equals custody control. [DEMONSTRATED/PLAUSIBLE]

**Practical effect:** QEPE materials must be written and routed with custody in mind. Not every seat-visible explanation is parent-company-safe. [DEMONSTRATED]

**Collapse mode if breached:** Custody failure — Academy loses ability to tell what was protected, shared, disclosed, or handed away. [PLAUSIBLE]

### 7.5 Seat Boundary

Seats may access QEPE materially enough to perform constitutional jobs, but what they disclose, summarize, or operationalize externally must remain bounded by review status, custody lane, and disclosure class. [PLAUSIBLE/IMPLEMENTABLE]

**Meaningful distinctions must remain between:** Full internal source material, internal strategic summary, patent-facing/filing-facing formulations, implementation notes, externally safe description. [DEMONSTRATED]

**Seat drift awareness:**
- ζ → constraint, pruning, adversarial challenge, reduction of unearned scope
- ω → embodiment, operationalization, scaling
- ξ → structured exploration, synthesis, possibility generation [PLAUSIBLE/DEMONSTRATED]

**Practical rule:** All three seats may participate in QEPE architectural work; none may unilaterally convert that access into external disclosure, governance mutation, or new custody posture. [IMPLEMENTABLE]

**Collapse mode if breached:** Role bleed — one seat speaks outside lawful review function, or all treat internal access as permission for broader disclosure. [PLAUSIBLE]

### Boundary Test

Every future QEPE move must pass:
1. Does it preserve the distinction between authority and exploration?
2. Does it preserve the distinction between tool and governance?
3. Does it preserve the distinction between demonstrated and desired?
4. Does it preserve the distinction between seat access and external disclosure?

If any answer is unclear, the move should slow down before it scales.
